Philosophy of Work

I believe in working smarter not harder and I think all programmers should strive for laziness. Let me further explain so you don't think I just want to be a bum. I believe the more I can automate my software to make mine and my coworker's lives easier the happier everyone is and the less we all have to work. Which brings me to my next point, I believe firmly in a work life balance. I know the silicon valley engineers are looking down at me becuase I don't want to code 100 hrs a week but I do because there is a lot more to life than working in an office all day. Lastly, I believe that programmers (and anyone for that matter) need long periods of interupted time to maintain flow state and the best creations are made when we can maintain this state each day. Hence, this is why it's a secret goal of mine to one day work remotely again. Don't get me wrong, I love where I work and the people I work with but the office environment is quite distracting and I know that my best work awaits when I get back to working in personal office again. NOTE: prior to MadWire I worked remotely for 4 years but you can read more about that in my resume.
